TURTLES

Sri Lanka is fortunate to have five of the seven live sea turtle species documented in the globe reported along the lovely coastline belt of the island - arriving for breeding.Reptiles belonging to the family Testudines, turtles have bony shells covering their bony body. Turtles come in more than 350 different species.


Sea turtles are fighting for survial

These ancient reptiles are primarily threatened by widespread poaching and over-exploitation, and they frequently become bycatch casualties.The species are negatively impacted by the increasingly erratic global climate, which alters the sand temperatures that their hatchlings depend on. All seven species have received a classification from Vulnerable to Critically Endangered by the International Union for Conservation of Nature.


Marine turtle are mating

In the shallow offshore seas, turtles mate primarily during the day. Others mating on the water's surface, while some turtles do so underwater. To grasp the female securely while mating, adult male turtles have strong claws. During the mating season, one female can have multiple matings with different males and store the sperm in her body. A single clutch may contain eggs that were fertilized by multiple guys since these stored sperm are used to fertilize eggs. After finding the nesting location, the female waits until dusk in the shallow waters near the shore. Because it is cooler and less visible to huge predators at night, female turtles choose to lay their eggs there.


Nesting Behaviour

Under the stars and the moon, sea turtles frequently lay their eggs at night.
